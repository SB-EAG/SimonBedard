# Simon's objectives

Personal and strategic objectives that guide how we prioritise and shape work. This is the private counterpart to the institutional records held in the organisation repositories.

## Overarching

Advance the interests of Simon, his family and Exit Advisory Group, while contributing genuine public good. These are not in tension. The contribution is real, and it also builds Simon's position.

## Profile and positioning

- Establish Simon as a credible, recognised industry voice on business succession and ownership transition.
- Build his standing as a capable board-level figure.
- His AIBB advocacy is unpaid and done in his own time and at his own cost. The return he seeks is profile, influence and reputation.
- Use his roles and participation, as AIBB Chair, through EAG, and in the advocacy work, to build that profile. Do this with subtlety, and without compromising the neutral, public-interest positioning those roles depend on, because that neutrality is what gives them their weight.

## Current major initiative

The advocacy for an SBA-style acquisition loan guarantee, paired with a succession-readiness education framework, led through the AIBB. The institutional plan lives in the AIBB repository's advocacy section.

Simon's personal aim within it is to be recognised as the driving force and convenor behind the agenda, and to build the relationships and profile that follow from leading a credible, national policy conversation.

The personal angle, the profile and the network being built, is tracked in `strategy/advocacy-positioning.md`. The institutional record lives in the AIBB repository under `advocacy/`.

## Exit Like an Expert (the book)

Simon's book, and his primary authority asset. It is the main call to action across all EAG and Buy Grow Sell content, and the credential behind his standing on business succession and ownership transition.

What it does for Simon's objectives:

- It is the authority engine. It establishes him as a recognised voice on succession and ownership transition, the positioning set out above, and it is the credential the AIBB Chair role already leans on.
- It is the top of the funnel. As the primary call to action across EAG and Buy Grow Sell, it is where most people first enter his world, ahead of any tool or conversation.
- It is compounding IP. Chapter 4's five drivers are the framework the Owner's Map is built on, so the book and the tool now reinforce each other. It also feeds the wider toolset, the Sweet Spot Matrix, the Negotiation Matrix and the Three-Circle Model.
- It scales his reach beyond his time. It carries his thinking and his name into rooms he is not in, which is what compounds profile and authority.

## The Owner's Map (productised IP)

EAG's owner-planning tool, a scored assessment that produces the MAP Score. The model is complete and the build spec is ready for Tom to build inside the valuation app. The institutional record, the model, scoring and build spec, lives in the Strategy repository.

What it does for Simon's objectives:

- It turns his own framework, the five drivers from Chapter 4 of *Exit Like an Expert*, into a working, scored tool. That deepens his authority on business succession and ownership transition, the positioning he is building.
- It makes EAG more scalable and more valuable. A repeatable, productised assessment is an asset in its own right and it lifts the firm's enterprise value, which serves the north star. Fitting for an adviser who helps owners build value ahead of a sale.
- White-label is deliberate. It opens an optional licensing line beyond EAG's own advisory work, other firms running the Owner's Map under their own brand.
- It works with the book. The book is the authority engine, the tool is the practical next step, and each points to the other.

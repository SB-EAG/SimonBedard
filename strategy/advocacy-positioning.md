# Advocacy positioning

Simon's personal angle on the small business advocacy work. The institutional record, the policy positions, the submissions and the engagement log, lives in the AIBB repository under `advocacy/`. This file is the personal counterpart: what the advocacy does for Simon's profile, and the network he is building through it.

Hold both truths. The public contribution is real and worth doing on its merits. It also builds Simon's standing, and that is a legitimate return on unpaid work done in his own time.

## The personal aim

To be recognised as the driving force and convenor behind a credible, national policy conversation on small business succession and acquisition finance. Not the loudest voice, the one others credit with having convened the agenda and brought the right people together.

The book, *Exit Like an Expert*, is the authority credential underneath this. The advocacy extends that authority from "the expert on exiting" into "the person leading the national conversation on it".

## How the agenda is tracking

The institutional detail is in the AIBB repo. In short, the agenda has moved from a Productivity Commission submission, to a letter from a federal MP to the Small Business Minister naming Simon, to warm co-advocacy from COSBOA, inside roughly ten months. That trajectory is the profile being built in real time.

The COSBOA call matters personally because it moves Simon from a single advocate into the convenor role: COSBOA supplies reach, the banks and the ABA can be brought in, and the AIBB, with Simon at its head, is positioned as the specialist authority the coalition forms around.

## The network being built

The advocacy is drawing people in. Relationships that now exist or are opening:

- **Dr Sophie Scamps MP**, who wrote to the Minister and made the state introduction.
- **Michael Regan MP**, state-level, who recognised the work publicly.
- **Skye Cappuccio (CEO, COSBOA)** and her policy lead **Adele**, now engaged.
- **Rebecca Warren (CBA)**, across the finance side.
- Introductions being offered to figures such as **Tim Wilson** and **Jane Hume**, given their background on this.

Simon's own read is that once he started pushing, people came out of the woodwork. That momentum is an asset. It should be cultivated deliberately.

## The guardrail

Simon's standing here depends on the AIBB's neutral, public-interest positioning. The moment the agenda reads as self-interest or as a partisan play, its weight drops, and so does the profile benefit.

So the personal profile is built by leading a genuinely credible, cross-party, evidence-based agenda, not by being seen to seek credit. The convenor role is claimed through the work, quietly. Keep the partisan calculations and the personal profile angle here, out of the institutional record.
